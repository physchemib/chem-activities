Task
You will analyse two molecules using structural reasoning. You must not only state answers, but justify them. Use:
  • Bond accounting: \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\)
  • VSEPR electron-domain counting (regions of electron density)
  • Hybridisation arguments (\(sp^3\), \(sp^2\), \(sp\))
  • Delocalisation/resonance where relevant
Reminder: single bond \(=\) 1 \(\sigma\); double bond \(=\) 1 \(\sigma\) + 1 \(\pi\); triple bond \(=\) 1 \(\sigma\) + 2 \(\pi\).
Molecule A — Methionine (amino acid)
Given (condensed): \(\mathrm{CH_3{-}S{-}CH_2{-}CH_2{-}CH(NH_2){-}COOH}\)
A1 — Bond accounting (show your method)
  1. Identify all multiple bonds present and name the functional group containing them (e.g. \(\mathrm{-COOH}\)).
  2. Deduce the number of \(\pi\) bonds.
  3. Determine total \(\sigma\) bonds. Briefly state how you counted (skeleton + X–H, etc.).
A2 — VSEPR: electron domains → geometry
  1. For S, N, and the carbonyl C: write the number of electron domains (bonding regions + lone pairs).
  2. State electron-domain geometry and molecular geometry for each.
  3. One sentence: why does a double bond count as one domain in VSEPR?
A3 — Hybridisation (justify)
  1. Assign hybridisation to: alkyl carbons vs the carbonyl carbon.
  2. Assign hybridisation to both oxygen atoms. Explain why they are not identical.
  3. Assign hybridisation to N and S (use domain counting + bonding).
A4 — Extension (higher complexity)
  1. Identify the stereocentre (if present) and justify using “four different groups”.
  2. Predict the most basic site (proton-accepting) and justify using lone-pair availability.
  3. Explain how protonation of the amine would affect geometry and hybridisation of N (use \(sp^3\) reasoning).
Self-check (A):

A1 Multiple bond: \(\mathrm{C{=}O}\) in the carboxylic acid \(\mathrm{(-COOH)}\). Therefore \(\pi\) bonds = 1. A complete correct count for the drawn molecule gives 19 \(\sigma\) and 1 \(\pi\).

A2 S: 2 bonds + 2 lone pairs = 4 domains → tetrahedral (EDG), bent (MG). N: 3 bonds + 1 lone pair = 4 domains → tetrahedral (EDG), trigonal pyramidal (MG). Carbonyl C: 3 domains → trigonal planar (EDG & MG). Double bond is one domain because VSEPR counts regions of electron density.

A3 Alkyl C: \(sp^3\). Carbonyl C: \(sp^2\). Carbonyl O is commonly treated as \(sp^2\) (pi framework), hydroxyl O as \(sp^3\) (two lone pairs + two \(\sigma\) bonds). N: \(sp^3\). S: \(sp^3\).

A4 Stereocentre: the \(\mathrm{CH}\) attached to \(\mathrm{NH_2}\), H, \(\mathrm{-COOH}\), and the side chain (four different groups). Most basic site: typically the amine N (lone pair more available than in the acidic \(\mathrm{-COOH}\) group). Protonation keeps 4 domains around N → still \(sp^3\) with tetrahedral domain geometry.
Molecule B — Aniline (phenylamine)
Aromatic ring + amine group: \(\mathrm{C_6H_5NH_2}\)
B1 — Aromatic bonding (delocalisation)
  1. How many \(\pi\) bonds are represented by the alternating double bonds in the ring?
  2. Explain why all ring \(\mathrm{C{-}C}\) bonds have the same length.
  3. Predict bond order/length relative to a pure single vs pure double \(\mathrm{C{-}C}\) bond.
B2 — Geometry & hybridisation (with nuance)
  1. State geometry and hybridisation of ring carbons and justify using electron domains.
  2. Predict geometry at N from VSEPR, then discuss whether the lone pair can overlap with the aromatic \(\pi\) system.
  3. Explain how delocalisation affects availability of the N lone pair for protonation.
B3 — Basicity & substituent effects (higher complexity)
  1. Compare basicity of aniline with an alkyl amine (e.g. ethylamine). Which is more basic? Why?
  2. Predict the effect of \(\mathrm{-NO_2}\) on basicity (electron withdrawing). Justify.
  3. Predict the effect of \(\mathrm{-OCH_3}\) on basicity (electron donating). Justify.

Self-check (B):

B1 The drawing shows 3 double bonds → 3 \(\pi\) bonds represented. In reality, \(\pi\) electrons are delocalised, making all \(\mathrm{C{-}C}\) bonds equivalent with bond order between 1 and 2 (length/strength intermediate).

B2 Ring carbons: 3 domains → trigonal planar, \(sp^2\). N: VSEPR suggests 4 domains → trigonal pyramidal shape, but the lone pair can overlap with the aromatic \(\pi\) system (partial delocalisation), reducing availability for protonation.

B3 Aniline is generally less basic than alkyl amines because the N lone pair is stabilised by interaction with the ring. \(\mathrm{-NO_2}\) decreases basicity (withdraws electron density). \(\mathrm{-OCH_3}\) increases basicity relative to aniline (donates electron density by resonance, qualitatively).
